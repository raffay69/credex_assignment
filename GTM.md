# GTM.md (Go-to-Market Strategy)

## The Exact Target User

The target user is not a generalized "founder," but specifically the **Fractional CFO**, **VP of Finance**, or **Director of Engineering/FinOps** at a Series A to Series C SaaS or tech-enabled services company (headcount 50-250). These are individuals tasked with reigning in operational expenses after a period of rapid, unmonitored growth where individual developers and teams expensed disparate AI tools without centralized oversight.

## The "Right Before" Moment

Right before they want this tool, they are:

- Staring at a chaotic monthly corporate credit card statement (Ramp, Brex, or Mercury).
- Googling queries like: "Claude Team vs Pro pricing," "GitHub Copilot Enterprise true cost," or "How to consolidate AI tool spend."
- Reading a Hacker News thread complaining about unexpected API overages from Anthropic or OpenAI.
- Prepping for a board meeting where they need to demonstrate cost-cutting measures without sacrificing engineering velocity.

## Where They Hang Out Online

- **Slack Communities:** FinOps Foundation Slack (specifically the `#cost-optimization` channels), and private CFO/Finance leadership groups like the 'Off the Ledger' Slack community (by Airbase).
- **Subreddits:** `r/SaaS`, `r/FinOps`, and specific technical subreddits where engineering leaders complain about costs, like `r/devops` or `r/ExperiencedDevs`.
- **Newsletters/X Lists:** Readers of "The Pragmatic Engineer" (Gergely Orosz) when he covers engineering costs, and followers of specialized FinOps influencers on X.

## First 100 Users in 30 Days ($0 Budget)

1.  **The "Teardown" Thread Strategy:** I will manually build 5 hypothetical "stack teardowns" for recognizable, mid-sized companies (e.g., "If Vercel uses X, they are overpaying by Y"). I will post these detailed teardowns on X and LinkedIn, ending with: _"I built a free calculator that does this exact math for your specific stack. Try it here."_
2.  **Targeted Cold Outreach (Value First):** I will scrape LinkedIn for "Director of FinOps" or "Fractional CFO" titles at Series A/B companies. Instead of a generic pitch, the cold message will be: _"Hey [Name], I noticed [Company] is scaling fast. I built a free, no-login tool that audits AI subscription bloat (Cursor, Copilot, ChatGPT). Takes 30 seconds to see if you're overpaying. Thought it might be useful for your next budget review."_
3.  **The Hacker News "Show HN" Launch:** I will launch on Hacker News on a Tuesday morning (optimal traffic). The title will be strictly functional: _"Show HN: I built a calculator to find where you're overpaying for AI tools (Cursor, Claude, OpenAI)."_ The comments will focus heavily on the defensible math and the fact that it requires no login to see the value.

## The Unfair Distribution Channel

The unfair advantage lies in **Credex's existing network and credibility.** If this tool is co-branded or sponsored by Credex, we immediately bypass the "trust barrier." I would leverage Credex's existing email list or client base. We send a dedicated email to current and past clients: _"We built an internal tool to audit AI spend. It saved us $X. We're open-sourcing the calculator today."_ Because Credex already has authority in the financial/credit space, users are highly likely to trust the tool and, crucially, book the high-savings consultation at the end.

## Week-1 Traction Metrics (If It Works)

If the viral loop and initial distribution hit the mark, Week 1 traction looks like:

- **1,500 Unique Visitors** (driven heavily by the Show HN post and initial X threads).
- **400 Audits Completed** (a high ~26% completion rate because there is no login friction).
- **60 Emails Captured** (15% of those who completed the audit opted into the lead gate for the PDF/notifications).
- **3 Qualified Consultations Booked** (from the high-savings cohorts prompted to speak with Credex).
- **50+ Shared URLs** circulating in private company Slack channels (the primary viral engine).
